What is the long term, and where does WinnerOdds define it?

In sports betting, the “long term” refers to the minimum number of bets required so that the expected results of a system aren’t solely based on luck (the so-called variance).

The more picks you place with a given system, the closer your results will align with its projected performance over time.

Long term According to WinnerOdds

At WO, we consider 1,000 bets to be our initial definition of the long term. Based on our betting history, the likelihood of being at a loss after 1,000 bets is very slim.

We set this benchmark to show new users that if they want meaningful results, they need to commit for the long haul and avoid giving up too soon.

It also helps us filter out users aiming to buy a Lambo within 2 weeks.

With a solid grasp of the system, most competent users should understand why reaching this initial milestone is crucial.

– If you’re not willing to stick around for at least 1,000 bets, WinnerOdds may not be the right fit for you.

You might be better off playing the lottery or visiting a casino instead.

Best of luck to you—whichever path you choose.
